The challenge

The moments utilities most need to communicate are exactly when their systems are most likely to fail.

01

Legacy IVR that collapses under surge

A major storm drives call volumes from hundreds to hundreds of thousands overnight. Legacy telephony systems queue-drop, agents are overwhelmed, and the organisation loses control of its most critical customer touchpoint at exactly the wrong moment.

02

Outage notifications that can't reach everyone

Mass SMS campaigns to millions of customers hit carrier throughput limits. Some messages are delayed by hours. Others are filtered. Customers call in to find out what they could have been told automatically, creating the surge your IVR just collapsed under.

03

Field communications that rely on phone calls

Engineer appointment confirmations, ETA updates, and access instructions go out by phone, tying up agents for calls that could be automated. Customers miss appointments because nobody told them the engineer was running late, and repeat visit costs mount up.

04

No audit trail during a major incident

Regulators want evidence of who was notified, when, and through which channel during a significant outage. Batch delivery reports don't answer that question. Without per-message records, incident reporting becomes reconstruction rather than reporting.
