The communications challenges retail and ecommerce operators face every day

01

Message queues collapse under peak demand

Black Friday, Cyber Monday, seasonal sales: the moments when you need flawless customer communications are the same moments your existing platform hits its limits. Single-carrier SMS campaigns throttle, queues back up, and order confirmation messages arrive hours late. Customers don't know if their purchase went through. Support calls spike. Trust erodes on the one day it matters most.

02

No governance across stores, depots, or regions

Distributed retail networks (hundreds of stores, trade depots, or regional fulfilment centres) all need to communicate with customers locally. But without central controls, local teams send off-brand messages, use unapproved channels, or generate compliance exposure. There's no visibility into what's being sent, no template enforcement, and no audit trail when something goes wrong.

03

Order and delivery notifications arrive too late, or not at all

Customers expect to know the moment their order ships, when it's out for delivery, and when it's been left. When those notifications lag, fail silently, or arrive out of sequence, inbound support volume rises and satisfaction scores fall. Most retail operations rely on a single SMS provider with no failover. One outage and thousands of customers are left in the dark.

04

CRM-triggered messaging requires too much manual effort

Transactional messaging that should be automatic (order confirmations, dispatch alerts, collection reminders, delivery windows) often requires manual campaign setup, CSV exports, or marketing team involvement. The gap between what the CRM knows and what the customer receives is filled with slow, error-prone human steps that don't scale as order volumes grow.
