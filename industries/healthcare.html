The communications challenges healthcare organisations face at scale

01

DNA rates that drain capacity and revenue

Did-not-attend rates of 5–15% are common across NHS Trusts and private healthcare providers. Most are preventable with the right reminder sequence. Phone-based outreach is too labour-intensive to scale; a single poorly-timed reminder message rarely changes behaviour, particularly without the ability for patients to confirm, cancel, or reschedule in the same channel.

02

Patient messaging that has to be both personal and compliant

Patient communications carry sensitive personal data subject to GDPR, NHS DSPT, and ICO requirements. Yet most messaging platforms treat healthcare organisations like marketing teams, with no data isolation, no audit trail, no control over where patient data is stored. Compliance teams are left retrofitting governance onto tools never designed for a regulated clinical environment.

03

Emergency alerts that need to reach everyone, fast

Public health events (pandemic notifications, urgent patient recalls, clinical safety alerts) demand the ability to reach large populations across multiple channels simultaneously. Most organisations discover the limits of their communications infrastructure at exactly the moment they need it to perform without fail.

04

Multi-site governance with no central visibility

NHS Trusts, integrated care systems, and private healthcare groups operate across dozens of sites, each running patient communications independently. Template versions drift. Consent handling varies. There's no way to enforce brand-safe, clinically-accurate messaging across the estate without restricting each site's ability to communicate with their own patients.
