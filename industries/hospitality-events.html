The challenge

You have won the booking. Now you have to keep the promise, while the floor is full and the phone keeps ringing.

01

The phone rings during service and nobody can answer

Hotels miss 100 to 140 calls a week. Restaurant lines go unanswered during the rush. Each missed call is a booking not taken or a change not made. Most callers who hit an IVR press zero, then abandon. The revenue walks before anyone picks up.

02

No-shows eat margin you cannot get back

No-shows hit 76 percent of venues, at roughly £3,621 per venue per year. A reminder with an easy way to confirm or rebook would recover most of it, but the reminders your booking tool sends are ignored, and there is no easy way for the guest to act.

03

Disruption turns into a scramble

A closure, a cancellation, a kitchen problem, a weather event: hundreds of guests need to be told, moved and reassured at once. A couple of staff message them by hand across separate tools while the phones light up. By the time everyone is told, it has changed again.

04

The guest journey is fragmented across tools

Confirmations from the booking tool, reminders from one app, marketing from another, and a shared inbox holding it together. No single view of what the guest has been told, no governance across venues, and no record of whether the promise was actually kept.
