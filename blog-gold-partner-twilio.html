Kaptea Achieves Twilio Gold Partner Status

9 Oct 2025 · 4 min read · Kaptea Team
The milestone

Kaptea has been awarded Twilio Gold Consulting Partner status, recognising our track record of delivering enterprise-grade communications infrastructure across regulated industries. More than 7 million messages delivered. More than 240,000 contact-centre conversations handled. Two flagship deployments that changed how organisations respond at scale.

Achieving Gold status in the Twilio partner programme is not a marketing exercise. It reflects a body of work: the number and complexity of deployments, the measurable outcomes delivered, and the depth of technical expertise demonstrated across real projects under real pressure.

For Kaptea, this milestone is the result of years of work with some of the most demanding clients in regulated industries. Healthcare providers, government bodies, financial services firms, and utilities operators who needed communications infrastructure that would not fail when it mattered most.

What Gold Partner status actually means

Twilio's partner programme has multiple tiers. Gold is awarded to consulting partners who meet strict criteria across technical certification, customer success, and revenue performance. It is not automatically renewed. Partners must maintain it through continued delivery quality.

For clients working with a Twilio Gold Partner, the practical implications are significant. Gold partners have access to deeper technical resources, priority support channels, and early access to platform capabilities. They have been vetted and approved by Twilio as organisations that can design, build, and operate enterprise-grade deployments.

For Kaptea specifically, the certification reflects expertise in Twilio Flex, Twilio Programmable Voice, SMS messaging, WhatsApp Business API, and the integrations that connect these channels to platforms like Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics, and custom enterprise systems.

The HSE: building Ireland's COVID-19 contact tracing system

In early 2020, Ireland's Health Service Executive faced an urgent requirement: build a contact tracing communications system capable of reaching every person who had been in contact with a confirmed COVID-19 case. It needed to work at scale, immediately, and with full data compliance under Irish and EU regulations.

Kaptea built that system. Working with the HSE, we designed and deployed a Twilio-powered communications platform that handled outbound notification, inbound response management, and agent-supported follow-up conversations. When case volumes surged, the system scaled to meet them. When protocols changed, the IVR flows and messaging scripts were updated within hours.

The project required building something new under conditions that allowed no margin for error. The stakes were clinical, not commercial. That experience shaped how Kaptea thinks about enterprise communications: reliability is not a feature. It is the baseline requirement from which everything else is designed.

A counselling platform that had to scale instantly

The second flagship deployment illustrates a different kind of challenge. A counselling and mental health support organisation approached Kaptea with a platform that had outgrown its infrastructure. What began as a service running on twelve agents needed to expand rapidly to serve a dramatically larger user base, across four countries, without disrupting ongoing sessions or compromising the sensitivity of the conversations taking place.

Kaptea rebuilt the platform on Twilio Flex, implementing intelligent routing, multi-channel intake (voice, SMS, and web chat), and a supervisor layer that gave team leaders visibility across all active sessions. The result was a platform that scaled from 12 to more than 120 agents across four countries, without a single reported session disruption during the transition.

The nature of the service meant that failure was not an acceptable outcome. Callers in distress could not be bounced between hold queues or dropped between channels. Every architectural decision was made with that constraint at the centre.

What this means for our clients

Gold Partner status does not change how we work. It confirms the standard we have already been holding ourselves to. But for organisations evaluating communications providers, it provides something concrete: an independent, Twilio-issued signal that Kaptea has the technical depth, delivery track record, and organisational capability to take on complex enterprise deployments.

We do not build things that look good in demos and fail in production. The HSE project, the counselling platform, every regulated-industry engagement we have completed: all of them were built with the same principle. Promises made must be kept.
