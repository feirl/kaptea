The foundation

Twilio sets the standard for cloud communications. Here's why we've chosen to build on it.

Global reach at carrier scale

Twilio operates in 180+ countries with direct carrier connections, local number provisioning, and PSTN access across every major market. Whatever region your customers are in, Twilio's infrastructure is already there, delivering with the reliability and throughput that enterprise operations demand.

99.95% uptime across channels

Twilio's cloud infrastructure is built for production workloads, redundant, distributed, and backed by a commercially binding SLA. For regulated industries where downtime isn't an option, that reliability is the baseline from which Kaptea adds its own resilience and failover layers.

Fully programmable APIs

Every Twilio product (messaging, voice, video, email, authentication) is exposed through clean, well-documented REST APIs. That programmability is what allows Kaptea to wrap Twilio in a governed enterprise layer without sacrificing any of the underlying flexibility or capability.

A product for every channel

Twilio's portfolio spans the full communications stack: Programmable Messaging, Programmable Voice, Twilio Video, SendGrid for email, Verify for authentication, and Flex for contact centre. Kaptea surfaces all of these through a single governed platform, so your teams don't need to manage multiple Twilio products directly.

Enterprise-grade security and compliance

Twilio holds ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II, PCI DSS, and HIPAA certifications. Data handling, encryption in transit and at rest, and audit controls are built into the platform. Combined with Kaptea's own compliance layer, regulated enterprises have a defensible, audit-ready communications stack from day one.

Trusted by 300,000+ businesses

From early-stage startups to Fortune 500 enterprises, Twilio is the world's most widely adopted cloud communications platform. The ecosystem of carrier relationships, partner integrations, and battle-tested infrastructure is the result of two decades of production workloads, and it's what every Kaptea deployment runs on.

The Kaptea layer

What Twilio doesn't provide, Kaptea does.

Enterprise governance that Twilio's APIs alone can't give you

Twilio's APIs are deliberately open and programmable, which is their strength. But for regulated enterprises, open access is exactly the problem. Kaptea wraps Twilio in a governance layer that controls who can send what, to whom, and through which channel. Template approval workflows, role-based access, division segmentation, and a full audit trail, all enforced at the platform level, not bolted on after the fact.

  • Role-based access control across every channel and division
  • Template creation, approval, and version-controlled publishing
  • Division-level guardrails: departments can only access their own data
  • Full audit log of every send, every edit, every login
  • Compliance enforcement without involving your engineering team
