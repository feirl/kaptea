Cyber Assurance

Cyber Essentials Plus

The UK government's cyber hygiene standard at the independently verified level. "Plus" means an external tester has audited the controls in practice, not just reviewed our answers to a questionnaire.

Five core technical controls (firewalls, secure configuration, access control, malware protection and patch management) validated by hands-on technical testing, not self-attestation.

Mandatory for all UK government contracts involving personal data or technical services. Required under Cabinet Office guidance and for suppliers to critical national infrastructure sectors.

Certificate renewed annually. Current status is verifiable in real time via the IASME certificate search; the dynamic badge confirms it has not lapsed.

Technical Security

CREST Accredited

The accreditation body formally recognised by the NCSC, the FCA and the Bank of England for technical security testing. CREST is how regulated sectors specify and verify the quality of security assurance work.

Independently audited across scoping methodology, testing execution, data handling, quality assurance and reporting standards, not a membership fee or self-declaration.

Recognised for CBEST assessments (the Bank of England and FCA's financial sector cyber resilience testing programme). Required under UK Procurement Policy Note PPN 014 for government security suppliers.

For financial services, healthcare and CNI buyers: CREST accreditation means any security testing work Kaptea commissions or delivers against your environment meets the standards your own regulator would expect.

Carrier Partnership

Telnyx Partner

A direct partner relationship with Telnyx's private global IP network. Where Twilio is Kaptea's primary platform, Telnyx is our carrier independence play: the infrastructure resilience credential that single-carrier platforms cannot offer.

Kaptea deployments can route across both Twilio and Telnyx infrastructure. No single-carrier dependency means no single point of failure, which is critical for utilities, emergency services and financial services.

Telnyx operates its own private global IP network, not aggregated third-party carrier routes. This gives differentiated latency and cost characteristics for voice-critical and high-volume messaging deployments.

Carrier diversity is an increasingly common expectation in regulated sector procurement. A Kaptea deployment is not dependent on any single provider's uptime, by design.

Security architecture

Certifications reflect controls, not claims.

The standards above exist because the underlying security controls are real. Here is what is in place across every Kaptea deployment.

Encryption

AES-256 encryption at rest. TLS 1.2+ in transit across all platform components and API connections. No exceptions.

Access control

Role-based access controls across every deployment. SSO support, two-factor authentication enforced at login, admin-only template and configuration controls.

Data residency

EU, UK and US residency options available as standard. Irish residency for Irish public sector customers. Data does not leave the agreed jurisdiction.

Incident response

Documented incident response procedures, tested and reviewed annually as part of ISO 27001 surveillance. Defined RTO/RPO for mission-critical deployments.
