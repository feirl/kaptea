The communications challenges transport operators face every day

01

Disruption alerts that arrive too late

When a service is cancelled or delayed, passengers often find out on the platform — not before they leave home. Operations teams rely on manual processes or disconnected tools to send notifications, and by the time alerts go out, the damage to trust is already done.

02

Carrier throttling kills high-volume sends

A major disruption — a nationwide strike, severe weather, or infrastructure failure — triggers a surge in outbound notification demand. Single-carrier SMS campaigns hit throughput limits. Messages queue, arrive late, or fail silently. Passengers remain uninformed exactly when they need information most.

03

Passenger and operational comms are siloed

Customer-facing alerts, driver dispatch, depot coordination, and logistics updates all run through separate platforms. When a disruption hits, teams scramble across tools — there's no single operational picture and no way to coordinate passenger communications alongside operational response in real time.

04

No audit trail for regulatory reporting

Regulators want to know when passengers were told, through which channel, and what they were told. Without per-message delivery records, operators can't evidence their notification obligations — leaving them exposed in the event of a complaint investigation or regulatory inquiry.
