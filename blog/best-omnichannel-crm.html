"Omnichannel CRM" is one of the most-searched terms in B2B software. The volume reflects a real problem. Most organisations have a CRM that holds customer records, a separate platform that sends marketing emails, a third tool that runs live chat, a fourth for SMS, and yet another for WhatsApp. The customer's data is fragmented across all of them. The customer's experience is fragmented across all of them. And nobody on the inside has the full picture.

An omnichannel CRM is supposed to fix that. The question this article answers is which platforms actually deliver, and how to choose between them.

What is an omnichannel CRM?

An omnichannel CRM is a customer relationship management platform that consolidates every channel a customer might use (email, chat, phone, SMS, WhatsApp, social media, sometimes in-app messaging) into a single timeline against a single customer record.

The distinction with a multichannel CRM is operational. A multichannel CRM offers many channels. An omnichannel CRM connects them. If a customer starts a conversation on chat, switches to email, and finishes on a phone call, the omnichannel CRM treats that as one continuous interaction. The agent on the call sees the chat and the email already in front of them. The customer doesn't have to repeat themselves.

The practical capabilities you should expect from a modern omnichannel CRM in 2026:

  • Unified customer record. One place where every interaction across every channel is tied to the same contact.
  • Channel orchestration. The ability to send a message on the right channel based on customer preference, behaviour, or business rules.
  • Automation and routing. Tickets, leads, and conversations routed to the right team or person automatically.
  • Reporting and analytics. Performance measured across the customer journey, not channel by channel.
  • Integrations. Native connectors to the tools your team already uses (Slack, Microsoft Teams, Shopify, Zoom, accounting platforms).
  • Compliance and audit. Especially in regulated sectors, the ability to retain, retrieve, and review communications across channels.

1. Salesforce

Best for: Enterprise sales and service organisations that need maximum depth and the broadest integration ecosystem.

Salesforce is the most widely deployed CRM in the world, with reporting, analytics, and customisation depth that no other platform matches. Sales Cloud, Service Cloud, and Marketing Cloud Engagement combine to cover the full revenue and customer-experience operation. Service teams get case tracking, SLAs, and self-service portals. AppExchange provides thousands of third-party apps that extend the core platform. Slack integration (since the Salesforce acquisition) connects the CRM directly into team communications.

Strengths

  • The largest CRM ecosystem and integration marketplace
  • Reporting and analytics depth used by Fortune 500 firms
  • Industry-specific clouds (Financial Services, Health, Manufacturing)

Weaknesses

  • Mobile feature parity lags behind desktop
  • Storage limits become a real cost at high volumes
  • Implementation complexity often needs partner support
Pricing Starter Suite from $25 / user / month. Pro Suite $100. Enterprise $165. Unlimited $330. Einstein 1 Service $500. Add-on modules (Marketing Cloud, Data Cloud) priced separately.

2. HubSpot

Best for: Mid-market revenue teams that want strong marketing and CRM alignment in one platform with a faster time to value than Salesforce.

HubSpot started as inbound marketing software and has grown into a full sales, service, and operations platform. The shared inbox unifies email, chat, and form submissions for support teams. Native survey tools and analytics dashboards capture customer feedback. The CRM is genuinely free at entry level, which keeps the door open for smaller teams to grow into the paid Hubs as they scale.

Strengths

  • Marketing automation and CRM in one well-integrated platform
  • Strong onboarding with extensive documentation
  • Built-in feedback forms and satisfaction surveys

Weaknesses

  • Per-seat costs scale fast on the paid Hubs
  • Reporting feels light at the enterprise tier
  • Customisation is more constrained than Salesforce
Pricing Free CRM with limited features. Professional Customer Platform from $1,300 / month. Enterprise Customer Platform from $4,700 / month. Pricing applies to bundled marketing, sales, and service hubs.

3. Freshworks (Freshsales)

Best for: SMB and mid-market teams that want a clean interface and built-in AI without Salesforce-level complexity.

Freshworks (originally Freshsales) covers ticketing, knowledge base, public forums, and multichannel messaging. AI features, branded as "Freddy AI," route messages, recommend follow-ups, and surface lead activity insights. The platform appeals to teams that want core CRM functionality with a short learning curve.

Strengths

  • Cleanest interface in the mid-market category
  • AI features built in, not an upcharge
  • Lower starting price than competing mid-market CRMs

Weaknesses

  • Phone support relies on third-party app integration
  • Customer support access is more limited at lower tiers
  • Customisation depth is less than Salesforce or HubSpot
Pricing Growth plan $11 / user / month. Pro plan $47 / user / month. Enterprise plan $71 / user / month.

4. Zendesk Sell

Best for: Sales-led teams already operating on Zendesk for support, who want pipeline management with the same vendor.

Zendesk Sell focuses on sales pipeline visibility with customisable, drag-and-drop dashboards, deal stage tracking, and contact data management. The "task player" keeps agents focused by surfacing full lead context for the next action. Native integration with Zendesk Support means cross-functional visibility for teams running both.

Strengths

  • Tight integration with Zendesk Support for service teams
  • Drag-and-drop dashboards that non-technical users can adjust
  • Strong messaging integration: WhatsApp, Microsoft Teams, others

Weaknesses

  • Performance dips reported under heavy concurrent load
  • Email automation is less developed than HubSpot or ActiveCampaign
  • Best value if you are already on Zendesk; less compelling stand-alone
Pricing Team plan $19 / agent / month. Growth plan $55. Professional plan $115.

5. ActiveCampaign

Best for: Marketing-first teams that want lifecycle automation depth and prefer behaviour-driven workflows.

ActiveCampaign focuses on automation. Hundreds of pre-built templates cover follow-up sequences, email cadences, and pipeline updates. The visual workflow editor is genuinely usable. Site tracking lets sales teams see what leads are doing on the website in near-real-time. Lead scoring sorts prospects by behaviour. Over 900 third-party integrations connect to most adjacent tools.

Strengths

  • Automation depth that most CRMs cannot match
  • 900+ integrations available out of the box
  • Strong site tracking and behavioural triggers

Weaknesses

  • Performance occasionally lags under heavy automation load
  • Email builder less polished than Klaviyo for visual brands
  • Reporting is functional rather than executive-grade
Pricing Starter $15 / month. Plus $49. Pro $79. Enterprise $145.

6. Klaviyo

Best for: E-commerce brands that want best-in-class email and SMS marketing with deep segmentation and Shopify integration.

Klaviyo is the e-commerce category leader for omnichannel marketing. It supports email, SMS, and push notifications across multiple languages and currencies, with 300+ e-commerce integrations including Shopify, BigCommerce, and Magento. Predictive AI suggests optimal send times, segments dynamic audiences, and forecasts customer lifetime value. Privacy compliance (GDPR, CCPA) is built in.

Strengths

  • Best-in-class segmentation depth and predictive AI for e-commerce
  • Industry benchmarks built into reporting
  • Cross-channel revenue attribution

Weaknesses

  • No native live chat or WhatsApp business support
  • Some advanced features lean on agency partner support
  • Strongest in e-commerce, less suited to B2B service teams
Pricing Free up to 250 contacts. Email plans from $45 / month. Email + SMS from $60 / month.

7. Omnisend

Best for: E-commerce teams that want pre-built automation across email, SMS, push, WhatsApp, and dynamic ads, fast.

Omnisend is a multichannel marketing platform purpose-built for e-commerce. It supports email, SMS, push, WhatsApp, pop-ups, and dynamic ads on Facebook and Google. Pre-built automation flows for cart abandonment, post-purchase, and win-back drop in with minimal configuration. AI features write email copy, predict customer actions, and review campaign performance. 130+ e-commerce integrations cover most online stores. Compliant with GDPR, CCPA, and TCPA.

Strengths

  • Pre-built automation flows that work out of the box
  • WhatsApp marketing native to the platform
  • Campaign Booster automatically retargets non-openers

Weaknesses

  • No native live chat
  • Some reporting is not real-time
  • Less suited to non-e-commerce sectors
Pricing Standard plan $11.20 / month. Pro plan $41.30 / month.

What to actually look for when choosing

The marketing pages all sound the same. Here is what matters when you sit down to evaluate against your actual situation.

The channels you genuinely use, not the ones you might

Every CRM lists 15+ channels. You probably operate 4 or 5. Pick the platform that handles your real channels well, especially the ones with regulatory or compliance weight (voice in financial services, SMS in healthcare reminders, WhatsApp anywhere with consent obligations). Pay less attention to features you will never use.

Integration with what you already have

The cost of switching to a new CRM is rarely the licence fee. It is the integration work to connect it to everything else you run: your accounting platform, your e-commerce store, your ticketing system, your data warehouse. The right CRM is the one with the highest-quality integrations to your existing stack, not the one with the most logos on the website.

Where compliance and audit live

If you are in financial services, healthcare, public sector, or any sector with a compliance officer, the CRM is one part of a wider regulated information chain. The questions to ask: Where is communication data archived? Who can retrieve it under what circumstances? How long is retention configurable? Where is the data physically held (EU, UK, US residency)? Most CRMs answer this for the channels they own natively, less well for channels they integrate to.

The total cost of running it, not the headline price

The starting prices in the comparison table above are entry-level. The real cost includes seat expansion as the team grows, the next-tier upgrade for the feature you actually need, the marketing automation add-on, the data warehouse connector, the implementation partner, and ongoing administration. Build a 3-year total cost of ownership before you sign a 1-year contract.

How fast you need to be in production

Salesforce can do almost anything. It can also take 6 months to configure properly. HubSpot is in production faster but has lower customisation ceilings. Freshworks gets you live fastest of any of them. The right answer depends on whether you have a deadline you can move or one you cannot.

Frequently asked questions

What is the best omnichannel CRM platform?

There is no single best omnichannel CRM. The right platform depends on team size, budget, sector, and the channels you actually use. For enterprise scale, Salesforce typically leads. For mid-market revenue teams, HubSpot. For small to mid-market teams that want clean UX, Freshworks. For e-commerce, Klaviyo or Omnisend. The seven platforms in this article cover the realistic shortlist for most buyers in 2026.

Which omnichannel CRM has the best integration with messaging channels like SMS and WhatsApp?

Most leading CRMs (Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics, Zendesk) handle SMS and WhatsApp through integration with a communications platform such as Twilio, rather than as a native channel. Integration quality matters more than which CRM you pick. Kaptea Pronto is built specifically as the operational layer that connects CRM systems to SMS, WhatsApp, voice, and email through Twilio's BSP relationship, with template governance and audit trails as standard.

What are the 4 C's of omnichannel?

Consistency, convenience, context, and customer data. Consistency means the customer sees the same brand and answer regardless of channel. Convenience means they can switch channels mid-conversation. Context means each interaction is informed by everything that came before. Customer data means a unified record across every channel.

What is the difference between omnichannel and multichannel CRM?

A multichannel CRM offers many channels but treats each as a separate stream. An omnichannel CRM links every channel into one continuous customer record, so conversations follow the customer rather than restarting on each new channel. Customers don't repeat themselves; agents have full context; reporting reflects the actual customer journey.

Which omnichannel CRM is best for enterprise sales teams?

Salesforce is the most common enterprise choice for the depth of customisation, ecosystem breadth, and reporting maturity. HubSpot has moved upmarket and is a serious contender for revenue teams that want a faster setup. The decision tends to be customisation depth (Salesforce) versus time to value (HubSpot). Both can be paired with Kaptea Pronto for governed SMS, WhatsApp, voice, and email orchestration.

Which omnichannel CRM is best for e-commerce?

Klaviyo and Omnisend are both purpose-built for e-commerce omnichannel marketing. Klaviyo leads on segmentation depth and Shopify integration; Omnisend is faster to set up with pre-configured automation flows. For e-commerce brands that need full sales and service CRM as well as marketing, HubSpot is the more common choice.

How much does an omnichannel CRM cost?

Entry-level plans start around $11 per user per month (Freshworks Growth, Omnisend Standard). Mid-market platforms range from $25 to $115 per user per month. Enterprise plans on Salesforce and HubSpot can reach $500 per user per month or higher with full feature sets. Communications platform costs (Twilio for SMS, WhatsApp, voice) are billed separately and are typically usage-based.
