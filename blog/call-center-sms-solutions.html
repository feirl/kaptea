Call centre SMS solutions: what enterprise teams should look for in 2026

12 Nov 2025 · 7 min read · Kaptea Team
The short answer

A call centre SMS solution is the infrastructure that lets your support and sales teams send and receive text messages at scale, with the same governance, audit, and CRM integration as voice. The right solution depends on volume, compliance posture, and how tightly you need it to integrate with your existing contact-centre platform. Most enterprise teams should be looking at Twilio-based platforms like Pronto rather than carrier-direct SMS APIs.

Why SMS still matters in the contact centre

Email gets ignored. Live chat closes the moment the customer leaves the page. SMS sits on the lock screen of every phone in the country and gets read inside three minutes on average. That is why SMS, after twenty years of being declared dead, is still the most reliable way to reach a customer when something is genuinely time-critical.

For contact centres, SMS handles three jobs that voice and email cannot do as well: confirmations and reminders, two-way short conversations that do not need a full call, and outbound notifications when a status changes. Done right, it offloads volume from the queue without degrading the customer experience.

What to look for in a call centre SMS solution

Most teams evaluating SMS for the contact centre look at price first. That's the wrong starting point. Per-message rates between providers vary by tens of cents at most. The real cost is in everything around the send.

  • CRM integration. The SMS conversation has to write back to the contact record. If your agent has to switch tools to see the SMS history, the integration has failed.
  • Two-way conversation handling. Inbound replies need to route to the right agent or queue, with full context. Without this, SMS becomes a one-way notification channel.
  • Template governance. Approval workflows for outbound templates, especially in regulated sectors, are not optional. Marketing should not be able to send unapproved content from your sender ID.
  • Audit and archive. Every message in and out, with timestamp, sender, recipient, and content version. Required in financial services, healthcare, and public sector.
  • Branded sender. A short code or alpha-sender that your customers recognise. Drives open and reply rates. Reduces fraud risk.
  • Failover. When SMS fails, the message should cascade to WhatsApp or email automatically. The customer should not be the one who finds out a message did not arrive.

Twilio versus direct carrier connections

For enterprise contact centres, the realistic choice is between a Twilio-based platform and a direct carrier or aggregator. Direct carrier connections used to be cheaper at very high volume. Twilio's pricing has closed that gap, and the platform features (programmable routing, multi-channel orchestration, compliance tooling) are now substantially ahead of what most carrier-direct integrations offer.

Compliance, opt-in, and the regulatory layer

SMS is regulated. In the US, TCPA dictates consent rules. In the UK, PECR and GDPR apply. In Ireland, the same plus sector-specific rules. Across the board, the requirement is clear: you need recorded consent before sending marketing SMS, you need an unsubscribe path, and you need to honour suppression lists across every channel and division.

An enterprise SMS solution should handle this at the infrastructure layer. Suppression lists should be enforced automatically before any send. Opt-out keywords (STOP, STOPALL, UNSUBSCRIBE) should be processed at the platform level, not by the agent who happens to be on shift. Per-message audit logs are needed for the moment a regulator asks.

How to measure if your SMS solution is actually working

Once SMS is live, the metrics that matter are not the ones the marketing platform shows you by default. Open rate is meaningless for SMS (almost everyone opens). Click-through is useful only for messages that contain links. The metrics that actually tell you the channel is performing:

  • Reply rate on outbound messages where reply is the desired action. Tells you whether the message landed and made sense.
  • Resolution rate on inbound conversations. Tells you whether SMS is offloading volume or just shifting it.
  • Time to first response. If you are below 5 minutes, customers stay engaged. Above 30 minutes, they have moved on.
  • Cost per resolved interaction. SMS should be cheaper than voice on a per-resolution basis. If it is not, something is wrong with routing or templates.
  • Compliance audit pass rate. Suppression breaches, consent gaps, archiving misses. These should be zero.

Frequently asked questions

What is a call centre SMS solution?

A call centre SMS solution is the infrastructure that lets contact-centre agents send and receive text messages alongside voice and other channels, with CRM integration, audit logging, template governance, and consent management. Modern enterprise SMS solutions are typically built on top of Twilio, with platforms like Kaptea Pronto adding the orchestration and compliance layer.

How much does an enterprise SMS solution cost?

Per-message rates vary by destination but typically run between $0.005 and $0.04 per SMS in the US, UK, and EU, with bulk volume discounts. The platform layer (CRM integration, governance, agent UI) is usually a fixed monthly fee. Kaptea Pronto starts at $1,500 per month for the platform with carrier costs billed separately through your own Twilio account.

Do I need consent before sending SMS to customers?

Yes. In the US, TCPA requires explicit prior consent for marketing SMS. In the UK and EU, PECR and GDPR require both a lawful basis (typically consent or legitimate interests) and a clear unsubscribe path. Transactional SMS (order confirmations, appointment reminders) sits under different rules in some jurisdictions but should still respect customer-initiated opt-outs.

Can SMS replace voice in the contact centre?

For some interactions yes, for others no. SMS is excellent for confirmations, reminders, and short two-way exchanges. It is poorly suited to complex troubleshooting, emotionally sensitive conversations, or anything where tone of voice matters. The right approach is to use SMS to deflect the calls that should never have been calls in the first place, and let voice handle what voice handles best.
