Optimising customer communication: Twilio SMS integrated with Microsoft Dynamics 365

3 Dec 2025 · 6 min read · Kaptea Team
The short answer

Adding Twilio SMS to Microsoft Dynamics 365 turns a contact record into a two-way conversation hub. Done well, every send and every reply writes back to the record automatically, suppression and consent are enforced in the CRM, and the audit trail covers every message. Done badly, you end up with a custom integration that drifts the moment Microsoft or Twilio ships an update.

Why connect Twilio SMS to Microsoft Dynamics 365

Microsoft Dynamics 365 is the system of record for customer relationships in many enterprise organisations, especially in financial services, public sector, and regulated industries. It holds the contact, the case, the consent, and the history. Twilio is the carrier-grade messaging infrastructure that actually moves SMS, voice, WhatsApp, and email at scale.

Without an integration, the two operate in parallel. Marketing schedules SMS campaigns in one tool, agents handle replies in another, and the CRM sees none of it. The customer's record never reflects what was actually said.

Three ways to integrate (and which one to pick)

There are three common approaches to connecting Twilio SMS to Dynamics 365. They are not equivalent.

  • 1. Custom Power Automate flows. Lightweight, fast to build, but breaks easily and rarely covers compliance, suppression, or audit properly. Fine for prototypes.
  • 2. AppSource connector apps. Several third-party apps exist. Quality varies. Some handle basic send; few handle governance, opt-in management, or two-way conversation routing well.
  • 3. A purpose-built communications platform on top of Twilio. Kaptea Pronto plugs into Dynamics 365 with two-way sync, opt-in management enforced in the CRM, and per-message audit. The communications layer becomes a first-class part of the Dynamics estate, not a side process.

What a good Twilio + Dynamics integration actually does

The technical functionality you should expect from a properly built Twilio-Dynamics integration:

  • Bidirectional sync. Outbound SMS triggered from a Dynamics workflow appears as a Note or Activity on the contact. Inbound replies create or update the corresponding record automatically.
  • CRM-driven sending. Dynamics workflows (case status change, appointment reminder, payment due) trigger SMS sends programmatically without a marketer touching anything.
  • Suppression enforced in Dynamics. Opt-out fields on the contact record are honoured before any send. Suppression lists do not live in a separate tool.
  • Template approval workflow. Outbound message templates are approved within Dynamics or its integrated approval system, with version history.
  • Per-message audit log. Every send, retry, failure, and reply timestamped against the contact for regulator review.

The compliance layer is not optional

Microsoft Dynamics 365 is widely used in regulated sectors for exactly the reason that it captures consent, audit, and case history natively. Adding SMS without preserving those properties undoes a lot of why Dynamics was chosen in the first place.

A proper Twilio + Dynamics integration must enforce consent on every send, retain audit-grade message logs, and surface message content to compliance review tools. This is not the kind of thing you want to discover is missing in the middle of an FCA inspection.

Frequently asked questions

Can I send SMS directly from Microsoft Dynamics 365?

Not natively. Dynamics 365 supports email out of the box, but SMS requires either a connector app from Microsoft AppSource, a custom Power Automate flow, or a purpose-built communications platform layer like Kaptea Pronto. Each has different operational and compliance trade-offs.

How does the integration handle inbound SMS replies?

A well-built integration routes inbound replies to the relevant Dynamics record automatically, either by matching the sender phone number to a contact or by lookup in a campaign or case context. The reply appears as a Note or Activity on the record, and triggers any defined Dynamics workflow (case open, agent notification, automated response).

Is this approach compliant with GDPR and Irish data protection rules?

Yes, when configured correctly. Microsoft Dynamics 365 is GDPR-compliant when used appropriately. Twilio offers EU data residency. Kaptea Pronto runs on a dedicated instance with EU, UK, or US data residency configurable per deployment, and provides per-message audit logs that satisfy GDPR record-keeping obligations.

How long does it take to integrate Twilio SMS with Dynamics 365?

A custom Power Automate prototype can be live in days. A production-grade integration with proper suppression, audit, and bidirectional sync typically takes weeks if built bespoke.
