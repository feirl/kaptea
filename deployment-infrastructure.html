Common questions

How long does a standard Pronto deployment actually take?
Most standard deployments go from contract signature to live traffic in under a week. The five-phase implementation covers discovery, instance provisioning, integration, testing, and go-live. Complex deployments with full CRM integration, SSO, and multi-division configuration typically complete within eight weeks, followed by a 30-day HyperCare period with elevated SLAs.
What does "no engineering team required" actually mean in practice?
For standard deployments, Kaptea's onboarding team handles everything: instance provisioning, channel configuration, user roles, division structure, SSO setup (Azure AD, Okta, OIDC/OAuth2), CRM connector configuration, and event streaming. There is no code to write on your side. If you want to use the open API or build custom integrations, that's available - but it's entirely optional and on your own timeline.
Do we need to move away from Twilio to use Pronto?
No. Pronto is a Bring Your Own Carrier platform. You connect your existing Twilio or Telnyx account credentials and Pronto adds the orchestration and governance layer on top. Your numbers, carrier rates, and contractual relationships stay exactly as they are. You don't migrate anything.
Can we choose between EU, UK and US data residency?
Yes. Residency is selected during the discovery phase and confirmed at provisioning. EU instances are deployed in AWS or Azure EU regions; US instances in AWS or Azure US regions. Because every Pronto deployment is a dedicated single-tenant instance, your data is never co-located with another customer's data regardless of which region you choose.
What certifications does Kaptea hold?
Kaptea holds ISO 27001:2022 certification, operates on SOC 2-compliant infrastructure, has a GDPR Data Processor Agreement in place, and holds Cyber Essentials Plus and CREST accreditation. These certifications are maintained continuously - not acquired for individual deals.
What happens after go-live - who manages the platform?
Kaptea manages the platform entirely after go-live. This includes applying security patches, managing Twilio API changes, handling carrier support tickets, monitoring delivery rates, and responding to incidents. Customers receive a dedicated success pod covering platform support, carrier operations, and compliance assistance. You focus on communications - we focus on infrastructure.
