Architecture

Every customer gets their own instance.
Not a seat in someone else's.

Kaptea does not operate a shared multi-tenant environment where your data sits alongside other customers' data. Each deployment is an isolated Pronto instance, with its own infrastructure, its own configuration, its own access controls. That choice has four direct consequences for compliance.

01

No data co-mingling

Your contact data, message history and configuration are completely isolated. No other customer's data is in your instance and none of yours is in theirs.

02

Configuration locked to your requirements

RBAC, retention policies, open-hours controls, template governance: all set to your standards. No shared defaults that another customer's configuration could affect.

03

Specific answers for your DPO

Where is data stored? Who can access it? How is it encrypted? The answers are specific to your instance, not approximations based on a shared environment.

04

Incident scope is contained

If an issue occurs in another customer's deployment, it does not affect yours. Isolation is structural, not a policy commitment.

GDPR

GDPR by design,
not by documentation.

GDPR compliance is not a feature Kaptea adds for regulated customers; it is how the platform is built. For Irish and EU customers, every decision about data storage, access and retention was made with the Regulation in mind from the start.

Standard Data Processing Agreement provided

A GDPR-compliant DPA is included with every deployment. Kaptea acts as data processor; the customer acts as data controller. Roles, obligations and sub-processor details are documented.

Configurable data retention, down to 90 days

Retention periods are set at deployment and enforced automatically. Contact data, message logs and delivery records are purged on schedule, not on request.

Right to erasure process documented

Data subject deletion requests are handled within the timescales required by the Regulation. The process is documented and available to your DPO on request.

No data used for training or third-party analytics

Customer data processed through Pronto is not used for model training, platform improvement analytics, or shared with any third party beyond documented sub-processors in the DPA.

Twilio sub-processor relationship managed by Kaptea

Kaptea manages the data processing addendum with Twilio as a sub-processor. Customers do not need to negotiate this separately or understand the underlying carrier relationship.

Who does what under GDPR

Data Controller

Your organisation

Determines the purposes and means of processing. Responsible for lawful basis, privacy notices and data subject rights.

Data Processor

Kaptea

Processes personal data on your behalf, under your instruction. Bound by the DPA. Appoints sub-processors only as documented.

Sub-processor

Twilio (and documented others)

Infrastructure and carrier services used by Kaptea to provide the platform. Listed in the DPA. Subject to Twilio's own DPA with Kaptea.

Access & security controls

The controls that stop human error
becoming a compliance incident.

Certifications confirm the framework. These are the specific controls in every Pronto deployment that make the framework real.

Role-based access control

Every user is assigned a role that determines exactly what they can see and do. Roles are defined at deployment and can only be changed by administrators.

SuperAdmin Admin User Read-only

Two-factor authentication

2FA is enforced at login across all platform access points; there is no opt-out path for users. For high-assurance deployments, hardware key support is available.

Enforced by default No opt-out

Single sign-on (SSO)

SSO integration removes password friction while enforcing your organisation's identity policies. Supports SAML 2.0 and OAuth 2.0 identity providers.

SAML 2.0 OAuth 2.0

Admin-only template controls

Message templates can only be created or edited by administrators. Operators can select and send from approved templates; they cannot modify them mid-send or create unapproved content.

Brand-safe Compliance-enforced

Open-hours controls

Campaigns can only be scheduled and sent within configured time windows. A campaign manager cannot accidentally trigger a send outside permitted hours, no matter what time they are logged in.

Configurable per instance

Audit logs

All platform activity is logged: who accessed the system, what they did, when. Audit logs are available to administrators and can be exported for compliance reporting and GDPR accountability obligations.

Exportable Tamper-evident

Encryption

AES-256 encryption at rest across all data stores. TLS 1.2+ in transit for all API connections and platform communications. SMS number obfuscation available for operators who must protect passenger or customer mobile numbers.

AES-256 at rest TLS 1.2+ in transit SMS obfuscation

Platform self-service controls

Administrators can pause campaigns, shut down channels and remove access in real time without contacting Kaptea. For mission-critical environments, you need the red button, not a support ticket.

Instant channel pause No ticket required

Carrier-level redundancy

Deployments can route across both Twilio and Telnyx infrastructure. No single-carrier dependency means no single point of failure at the communications layer, a requirement in several regulated sectors.

Twilio Telnyx Multi-carrier failover

Incident response

When something goes wrong,
you hear about it first.

Documented procedures, named contacts, defined response times. For organisations using Pronto for mission-critical communications, uptime is not a performance metric; it is a service requirement.

1
Detection and classification

Issues are detected through monitoring and classified by severity (P1, P2, P3) within defined timeframes. The classification determines the response path and escalation chain.

2
Named point of contact activated

A named Kaptea contact is assigned to the incident. Customers are notified directly, with no ticket queue and no generic support inbox for P1 and P2 issues.

3
Root cause identified and communicated

The cause is identified, not just the symptom. Customers receive a clear explanation of what happened, what was done and what is in place to prevent recurrence.

4
Post-incident review

All P1 incidents receive a written post-incident review. For deployments with an Enterprise support agreement, reviews are provided within five business days.
