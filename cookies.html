1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, improve user experience, and provide information to website owners.

Cookies are stored in your browser and sent back to the originating website on subsequent visits. They can be "first-party" (set by us) or "third-party" (set by services we use, such as analytics providers).

Cookies can be "session cookies" (deleted when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (which remain on your device for a defined period or until you delete them).

2. How we use cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Ensure the website functions correctly and securely
  • Remember your preferences during and between sessions
  • Understand how visitors navigate and interact with our website
  • Measure the effectiveness of our content and marketing
  • Improve the experience for future visitors

First-party cookies we set enable core website operations and do not collect personal data that identifies you individually. Third-party cookies (where you consent) track performance, interactions, and security.

3. Types of cookies we use

Essential
Required for the website to function. They include session management and security cookies. These cannot be disabled without impairing website functionality. No consent is required for essential cookies under applicable law.

Statistics
Help us understand how visitors use our website by collecting information anonymously (such as visitor counts, unique visitors, pages visited, and traffic sources). This data helps us improve our content and navigation.

Functional
Enable enhanced features such as embedded video content and social media sharing. These cookies may be set by third-party providers whose services appear on our pages.

Preferences
Allow the website to remember your choices (such as language, region, or display settings) to provide a more personalised experience on return visits.

Marketing
Used to deliver relevant advertisements and measure the effectiveness of campaigns. These cookies may track your activity across other websites. We only deploy marketing cookies with your explicit consent.

6. Managing your preferences

You can control cookies in the following ways:

Browser settings: Most browsers allow you to refuse some or all cookies, delete existing cookies, or be notified when a cookie is set. Consult your browser's help documentation for instructions. Note that disabling essential cookies may impair website functionality.

  • Chrome
  • Firefox
  • Safari
  • Edge

Opt-out tools: You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking across all websites using the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.

Do Not Track: Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" signal. Our website currently does not alter its behaviour in response to Do Not Track signals, though we continue to monitor regulatory guidance on this.
