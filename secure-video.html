The problem

Consumer video tools weren't built for regulated enterprise use.

Recordings in the wrong place

Consumer video platforms store recordings on their own shared infrastructure. For regulated industries (healthcare, financial services, legal), that means sensitive conversations sitting on servers you don't control and can't audit.

No access controls that scale

Meeting links forwarded to the wrong person. No passcode enforcement. No admin visibility over who joined, when, or what happened on the call. Consumer tools are designed for convenience, not governance.

Isolated from your comms platform

Video happens in one tool, voice in another, messaging somewhere else entirely. There's no shared record of the interaction, no connection to CRM, and no way to trigger follow-up communications from what was discussed on the call.

How it works

Secure, compliant, and fully auditable: from first join to final recording.

Twilio Video infrastructure. Zero software installs for anyone.

Kaptea Secure Video runs entirely in the browser, built on Twilio Video's carrier-grade WebRTC infrastructure. Participants join via a secure link and passcode in Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge. No app download, no account registration, no plugin: just click, pass security, join.

  • Browser-based video: Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge supported
  • Passcode authentication per meeting, no open links
  • Camera, microphone, and speaker controls in-meeting
  • Screen sharing with optional disable per meeting configuration
  • Virtual background blur and image replacement
  • In-meeting chat via Twilio Conversations (configurable per meeting)
  • Dial-in via Twilio Voice for participants without browser access
  • Dial-out to invite participants by phone from within the meeting

Automatic recording, secure S3 storage, AI transcription.

When recording is enabled, Twilio composes the meeting and delivers it to Kaptea Secure Video, which uploads the file to your dedicated AWS S3 bucket, not shared storage, not a consumer cloud. Recordings are accessible via CloudFront signed URLs with no direct S3 exposure. AWS Transcribe converts every recording to searchable text.

1
Recording enabled: configured per meeting; recording indicator shown to all participants in-room
2
Twilio composes: Twilio produces a composed MP4 and sends a composition-available webhook to Kaptea Secure Video
3
Upload to your S3: Kaptea Secure Video downloads from Twilio and uploads to your dedicated S3 bucket; PostgreSQL updated with composition metadata
4
Secure playback: recordings accessible via CloudFront signed URLs from the admin dashboard; no direct S3 exposure
5
AI transcription: AWS Transcribe processes the recording; full searchable transcript available alongside the recording

Pre-call diagnostics that eliminate "I can't connect" before the meeting starts.

Kaptea Secure Video ships a dedicated pre-call diagnostics application (separate from the video app) that participants run before joining high-stakes meetings. It checks browser compatibility, camera and microphone access, network connectivity, and Twilio service status. Support teams can direct anyone with issues here before escalating.

  • Browser and device compatibility checks
  • Camera and microphone access and quality verification
  • Network connectivity and packet loss testing
  • Twilio service status display (Video, Recordings, Compositions)
  • Anonymous technical issue reporting to the Secure Video API for support
  • Shareable diagnostics link: send to participants ahead of a meeting
  • Reconnection handling with automatic recovery inside the meeting room

All video is encrypted in transit via Twilio's SRTP/DTLS implementation. Meeting links cannot be forwarded and used without the passcode. Admin dashboard access is controlled via OAuth or SSO.
