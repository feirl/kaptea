The communications challenges public sector organisations face at scale

01

Not every citizen uses the same channel

Government communications can't assume everyone has a smartphone, an app, or checks their email. SMS reaches virtually every mobile device. WhatsApp reaches billions. Voice reaches those who respond to nothing else. Public sector organisations relying on a single channel — typically email — routinely fail to reach the citizens who need their communications most.

02

Emergency alert infrastructure that's untested until it's needed

Civil contingency events — severe weather, public safety incidents, national health crises — demand the ability to reach entire populations simultaneously across every channel. Most organisations discover the capacity limits of their communications infrastructure at exactly the moment those limits matter most. The time to find out is not during a live emergency.

03

Departments communicating independently with no governance

Across central government, local authorities, and arm's-length bodies, teams send citizen communications independently — with no shared template library, no consistent messaging, and no central visibility. Compliance officers can't see what's going out under the organisation's name. When something goes wrong, there's no audit trail to reconstruct what happened.

04

Public accountability demands evidence, not estimates

FOI requests, ICO investigations, and public inquiries increasingly require specific evidence: exactly when citizens were notified, through which channel, and what they were told. Assembling this from multiple disconnected systems — when it's possible at all — takes days and produces incomplete records. That's a governance failure, not just an operational inconvenience.
