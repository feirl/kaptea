The challenge

Generic AI agents don't pass compliance review. Heavy-setup AI builders never make it past the proof of concept.

01

The agent says things you can't defend

Out-of-the-box AI agents follow the LLM's defaults, not your brand voice, your compliance posture, or your contractual obligations. One off-script answer and the next conversation is with a regulator.

02

Setup is a project, not a session

Most AI builders need engineering, training data, model wiring, telephony integration, and a vendor implementation team before the first real call. By then the use case has moved on.

03

The AI is only as useful as what it can do

Talking to a customer is the easy part. Booking the meeting, looking up the order, raising the ticket, capturing the right fields, that's where most AI deployments quietly stop.

04

Handoff is where AI fails

When the AI hits its limit, the customer either gets stuck in a loop, dropped to voicemail, or routed to a human agent with zero context. Most AI deployments never solve the handoff.

Master prompts: your script, governed

The Agent tab gives compliance and operations teams the controls they need. Master prompts define what the agent says, what it must never say, what data to consult, and when to escalate. Style rules ('Respond in one or two short sentences. Never more than 25 words per turn') sit alongside the system prompt. Placeholders inject live customer context. Versioned, approved, audited.

  • Master prompt with style rules and {variable} placeholders
  • Per-assistant model, temperature, and max tokens
  • Active or paused state, tunable greeting per assistant
  • Versioned and approved through the same governance flow as message templates
  • One assistant, one master prompt, one audit trail

Tools, data capture, and knowledge bases

Three tabs turn the assistant from a chat bot into a working agent. Tools are function definitions exposed to the LLM at runtime, calling Pronto, your CRM, or any webhook. Data Capture pulls structured fields out of the conversation and writes them to the call record. Knowledge bases give the assistant retrieval-augmented context from your URLs, documents, and markdown.

  • Tools: webhook, Pronto, and CRM function calling exposed to the LLM
  • Data Capture: key, label, source, required, persisted on the call record
  • Knowledge: ingest URLs, documents, markdown, automatic or tool-call retrieval
  • Tunable retrieval: Top-K chunks, score threshold, max context tokens
  • 'What the assistant saw' debug view of retrieved chunks per call

Live escalation into Pronto with full context

The Escalation tab defines triggers: a sentiment threshold (e.g. negative), trigger keywords ('speak to a human', 'agent please', 'representative', 'manager'), or intent detected by the LLM. When fired, the call lands as a voice task in a Pronto agent inbox queue. The agent picks up with the transcript, AI summary, sentiment timeline, and event log already on screen.

  • Sentiment-threshold escalation with configurable values
  • Editable trigger keywords per assistant
  • Routes to a named queue in Pronto, or auto-matches by channel
  • Full transcript, summary, sentiment, and event timeline on the agent screen
  • Twilio Intelligence operators run on every call (Sentiment, Escalation Request, PII Extraction, Conversation Summary, plus custom operators)

Conversational AI: what you need to know

Pronto sits as the orchestration and governance layer on top of Twilio Conversation Relay and Twilio Conversation Intelligence. The customer connects their Twilio account through a Pronto voice channel; Pronto provisions a Conversation Intelligence service inside it, configures the operators, and exposes the assistant configuration through nine tabs (Agent, Voice, Tools, Data Capture, Knowledge, Escalation, Phone Number, Intelligence, Conversations). The carrier-grade voice and CI infrastructure stays with Twilio. The control plane, master prompts, tools, and audit layer sit in Pronto.
Six templates ship with Pronto: Blank (start from scratch with a neutral prompt), Appointment Scheduling (books, reschedules, and cancels appointments), Customer Service & Support (front-line agent that triages), Information Collector (systematically gathers required fields like name, account number, reason for call), Lead Qualifier (B2B discovery agent), and Surveys & Feedback (rating, NPS, open-text). Templates pre-fill the master prompt, voice, tools, and data capture so a working assistant can be live in minutes.
Three layers of capability. Tools let the assistant call into Pronto, your CRM, or any webhook at runtime, the function definitions are exposed to the LLM so the model picks the right tool to execute (book a meeting, look up an order, raise a ticket). Data Capture collects structured fields during the conversation (name, account number, booking time, reason) and persists them on the call record. Knowledge bases give the assistant retrieval-augmented context from your URLs, documents, and markdown so it can answer from your own content rather than the model's training data.
Escalation triggers come from three sources: a configurable sentiment threshold (e.g. escalate when sentiment falls below 'negative'), trigger keywords ('speak to a human', 'agent please', 'representative', 'manager', editable per assistant), or explicit intent detected by the LLM. When a trigger fires, Pronto routes the call into a Pronto agent inbox queue as a voice task. The agent picks up with the full transcript, AI-generated summary, sentiment timeline, and event log already on screen, with no need for the customer to repeat themselves.
Three TTS providers are available out of the box: ElevenLabs (default, with the largest voice catalogue), Google, and Amazon Polly. Voices are organised by language with a Test button that calls a phone number to play the voice live. Default STT is Deepgram with the Nova-3 general model, plus Nova-2 variants tuned for phone calls, meetings, finance, and medical contexts. Smart format auto-formats numbers, dates, and currency. Profanity filter masks profane words in transcripts. STT hints bias recognition toward product names, jargon, and regulated terms.
Pronto provisions a Twilio Intelligence service inside the customer's voice channel and attaches operators to it. Available operators include prebuilt ones (Sentiment Analysis, Escalation Request, Conversation Summary, PII Extraction, Voicemail Detection, Do Not Contact Me, Outbound Call Disposition, Recording Disclosure, Agent Introduction, Non English Call) plus custom generative operators authored in Pronto (e.g. an Agent Performance Score that rates the assistant on each call). Auto-redact PII, summary capture, and per-turn sentiment scoring run on every call by default.
