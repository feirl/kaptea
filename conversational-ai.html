The challenge

Generic AI agents don't pass compliance review. Locked-in AI vendors don't pass procurement review.

01

The agent says things you can't defend

Out-of-the-box AI agents follow the LLM's defaults, not your brand voice, your compliance posture, or your contractual obligations. One off-script answer, and the next conversation is with a regulator.

02

Locked into one model vendor

The model market is moving every few months. Picking a single AI vendor today and hard-wiring it into your customer comms is a procurement risk and a strategic one. You need optionality.

03

No live signal during the conversation

Sentiment shows up in a weekly report. By then the customer has churned, the regulator has been called, or the support queue has tripled. The intelligence has to be live, in the moment, actionable.

04

No clean handoff to a human

When the AI hits its limit, the customer either gets stuck in a loop, dropped to voicemail, or routed to an agent with zero context. The handoff is where most AI deployments quietly fail.

Master prompts: your rules, governed

Master prompts are versioned, approved, and audited instructions that govern how the AI behaves in any context. Define what the agent must say, must never say, when to escalate, what brand voice to use, what data to consult, and what is out of scope. Compliance owns the prompt. Engineering owns the integration. Both ship from the same console.

  • Version-controlled master prompts with approval workflow
  • Context-specific commands: different prompts per intent, per division, per channel
  • Off-script and brand-safety guardrails enforced at runtime
  • Per-prompt audit log: who changed what, when, and why
  • A/B safe-rollout for prompt changes
