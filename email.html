Email should be the easiest channel. So why does it keep going wrong?

Enterprise email breaks down at the intersection of volume, compliance, and cross-team governance.

No governance over who sends what

Marketing, ops, and support all have their own email tools. Brand consistency is a prayer, not a policy. One rogue send to a suppressed list and you're in the regulator's inbox.

Deliverability risk nobody owns

Bounce rates creep up. Suppression lists fall out of sync. You only discover there's a problem when open rates tank or the ESP flags your domain for review.

Email is an island

Your ESP doesn't know about the SMS you just sent, the WhatsApp reply you got, or the CRM status that should have stopped the send. Email without omnichannel context is just noise.

Compliance & Governance

Template approval workflows that match enterprise reality.

In regulated organisations, email content can't go out unchecked. Pronto enforces an approval workflow for every template change — ambassadors submit, administrators review, and nothing ships until it's cleared. Every action is audit-logged.

  • Template change requests require admin approval before use
  • Admin notification emails via SendGrid with direct review link
  • Full template audit log: created, changed, approved, rejected
  • Division-scoped templates — teams see only what's relevant to them
  • Role-based access controls on template creation and editing
  • Export audit log as CSV for compliance reporting

How approval works

When an ambassador creates or edits a template, it enters a pending state. Admins receive an instant email notification with a direct link to the review queue. The template cannot be used in a campaign until approved — protecting brand consistency and regulatory compliance at scale.
