The challenge

Multichannel isn't journey orchestration. Most stacks stitch four tools together and call it a journey.

01

Journeys that start strong, then quietly fail

The customer gets the email. They don't open it. Silence takes over until they call support. The journey stopped working at exactly the moment intervention was cheap.

02

Templates trapped across different tools

Marketing in one CRM, transactional in SendGrid, support in Zendesk. Three template engines, three approval flows, three audit trails. No single view of what the customer received.

03

No memory between channels

Each channel forgets what happened on the last one. The customer repeats themselves three times, the agent reconstructs context manually, and the journey loses momentum exactly when it shouldn't.

04

Frustration spotted only after the fact

Sentiment shows up in a weekly report, not in the live moment when an agent could fix it. By the time the dashboard updates, the customer has already churned or escalated.

Customer journey orchestration: what you need to know

Customer journey orchestration is the practice of running a complete, multi-step customer interaction (across channels, systems, and time) from a single platform. It goes beyond multichannel messaging by stitching together event triggers, conditional logic, channel selection, template population, and live agent escalation into one continuous workflow. In Pronto, a single journey can start with a commerce event, enrich data through API calls, dispatch on the customer's preferred channel, listen for sentiment in the response, and route to a live agent with full context if frustration is detected.
Pronto supports dual-payload journeys. A trigger can arrive with a full payload (all customer and order data pre-built), or with a minimal payload (just an identifier). On a minimal payload, Pronto calls your customer-data and order-data APIs in real time, populates the template with the live values, and dispatches on the chosen channel. This means commerce events, CRM updates, or partner systems can trigger journeys without having to build the message body upstream.
