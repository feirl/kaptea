The challenge

Financial services communications are high-stakes, high-regulation, and high-volume — all at once.

01

Fraud alerts that arrive too late

Seconds matter in fraud prevention. When an SMS fails or queues behind a marketing batch, the window to protect the customer closes — and liability opens. Most messaging platforms treat fraud alerts the same as promotional sends.

02

OTP and authentication messages filtered as spam

2FA and OTP messages are increasingly carrier-filtered in both directions. Failed verification means failed transactions, abandoned onboarding, and support queues that cost real money — with no automatic retry or channel fallback.

03

Regulatory notices without proof of delivery

FCA, PRA, MiFID II, and GDPR mandate that certain communications are delivered and evidenced. Batch delivery reports don't satisfy regulators. Without per-message audit trails, compliance becomes a hope rather than a guarantee.

04

Multiple divisions running separate tools

Retail banking, wealth management, insurance, and broker arms each run their own messaging stack. Customers receive duplicated or contradictory messages, brand controls break down, and no one has the full picture of what's been sent.
