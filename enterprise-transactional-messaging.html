Transactional communication: what you need to know

SendGrid is excellent for API-driven transactional email. Pronto sits on top of that layer and adds the things SendGrid intentionally doesn't: a drag-and-drop template editor with approval workflows, ad-hoc campaign capability for marketing teams, multi-channel dispatch (SMS, WhatsApp, RCS, Voice), and per-sub-account billing meter readings for intercompany cross-charging. You keep the regulated transactional backbone you already have. You add the orchestration, governance, and self-service tooling around it.
Yes. Pronto is designed to layer over an existing API-driven transactional setup. Your sender domains, authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC), and compliance posture stay where they are. Pronto plugs in as the orchestration, template management, and channel-extension layer. There's no rip-and-replace, and no disturbance to the regulated backbone you've already certified.
When enterprises move to a parent-sub account model on a provider like SendGrid for governance reasons, group finance often loses entity-level billing visibility. Pronto exposes per-sub-account meter readings (message counts, channel mix, period-based spend) via UI and API, so finance can cross-charge by operating company without manual reconciliation. The same approach works for Twilio sub-accounts and equivalent provider models.
Yes. Pronto's role hierarchy supports admin, approver, creator, and read-only roles, with division-, location-, or business-unit-scoped data. Central brand, compliance, and finance teams set the master templates, approval rules, suppression lists, and budget guardrails. Local teams (stores, depots, regional offices) execute campaigns within those rules. Both centralised and distributed retail patterns are supported by the same platform.
Transactional communications are triggered by an event (a transaction, an account change, a service update), and the customer expects them. Marketing communications are scheduled and require consent. Pronto separates the two with priority queue routing, distinct templates, and distinct audit trails, while keeping both on a single governance and billing platform. This means the regulated transactional layer is never disturbed by promotional traffic, and compliance can demonstrate the separation to a regulator.
Pronto handles both centralised and distributed models. In a centralised group (single brand, central marketing), one template library serves the whole organisation with role-based edits. In a distributed group (multi-store, multi-depot, multi-brand), the central library is locked, and each location or business unit gets a sub-library with customisation rights. Suppression lists, audit logs, and reporting roll up across the group regardless of model.
