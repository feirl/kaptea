Background

An Irish charity scaling food redistribution across the country

FoodCloud connects retailers and food businesses with surplus food to charities and community groups that need it. The model works at scale: thousands of pickups, thousands of recipients, real-time coordination between donors and beneficiaries.

Behind that operation is a contact centre. Donors ring to coordinate pickups. Charities ring to confirm collections. The advisor team is the connective tissue that keeps food moving from supermarket shelf to community plate.

The Challenge

A legacy phone system, two CRMs, and a global pandemic

The existing on-premise phone system worked, until it didn't. The pandemic hit. Agents needed to work remotely. The legacy system was tied to the office. And the team operated across two CRM systems: a legacy database and a newer Salesforce instance, with customer records spread across both.

FoodCloud needed a cloud-native contact centre, dual-CRM lookup so advisors could find any customer regardless of which system held their record, and a deployment that didn't require a large IT consulting engagement. They had a small team and a mission that couldn't pause.

The Solution

Twilio Flex, dual-CRM, and a Chrome browser

Kaptea deployed Twilio Flex as the contact-centre platform: cloud-based, programmable, accessible from anywhere. The harder problem was the dual CRM. Kaptea built a custom API that queries both the legacy database and the Salesforce instance simultaneously on every incoming call, returning a unified view of the customer regardless of which system holds the record.

Salesforce connectors handled the new CRM. Google Single Sign-On secured advisor access. The full stack runs through Chrome, with a headset. No specialised hardware. No on-premise install. No IT consulting team needed to operate it.

Twilio Flex deployment

Cloud-based programmable contact centre that runs on any modern browser. Remote-ready from day one, with multi-channel routing across voice, SMS, and web in a single agent interface.

Custom dual-CRM API

A custom API queries both the legacy CRM and Salesforce in parallel on every inbound call, returning a unified customer view regardless of which system holds the record. Advisors no longer have to guess where to look.

Salesforce + Google SSO

Native Salesforce connectors for the new CRM, paired with Google Single Sign-On for secure authenticated advisor access. The whole stack speaks the same auth identity, the whole way down.

Chrome + headset only

Deployment requirement: a Chromebook and a headset. No special hardware. No on-premise software. No IT field-engineer visits. Charity-budget operating costs, enterprise-grade contact centre.

Results

From legacy stack to surplus-food scale

FoodCloud now manages the redistribution of more than 8,000 tonnes of surplus food, the equivalent of 19.2 million meals, with a 20-person advisor team operating across four communication channels and tracking 15,000+ conversations.

  • Cloud contact centre deployed during the pandemic

    FoodCloud's contact centre went from on-premise legacy to fully remote on Twilio Flex without service interruption. Mission-critical food redistribution kept moving.

  • Dual-CRM unified at the agent screen

    Legacy and Salesforce records are queried in parallel on every call. Advisors see one customer, not two databases. The "which system are they in?" question disappears.

  • 20-person advisor team scaled on the platform

    The team grew on Twilio Flex without re-platforming, re-licensing, or rebuilding integrations. Capacity follows demand, not the other way around.

  • 15,000+ tracked conversations across 4 channels

    Voice, SMS, web, and email all routed and recorded through one platform. Donors and charities reach the team where they prefer; the advisor sees it all in one place.

  • 19.2 million meals enabled

    The contact centre is the operational backbone of redistribution. The technology disappeared into the work, exactly as charity-mission infrastructure should.
