Background

A national health emergency that needed communications infrastructure, fast

In early 2020, Ireland's Health Service Executive faced a public health emergency unlike anything the country had encountered in modern memory. The HSE needed to identify, notify, and follow up with every person who had been in contact with a confirmed COVID-19 case, in real time, at national scale, under full GDPR and Irish data protection compliance.

The contact tracing programme was being designed and operationalised in parallel with the technology that would carry its messages. Notifications had to reach test recipients, close contacts, and follow-up groups within hours of a confirmed case. Inbound queries had to route to trained tracers. Every interaction had to be logged, auditable, and resilient under volumes nobody had ever planned for.

The Challenge

Build it, scale it, change it daily, and never let it fail

Existing telephony and CRM systems were not built for this. The HSE needed a programmable communications layer that could be configured by health policy decisions, not by procurement cycles. When test result protocols changed, the IVR flows and SMS scripts had to update within hours. When case volumes surged, the platform had to scale without operational intervention. When clinicians needed to follow up with vulnerable contacts, the workflow had to route into a CRM that was already part of the wider HSE estate.

And the stakes were clinical, not commercial. A failed message wasn't a missed marketing opportunity; it was a person who didn't know they had been exposed.

The Solution

Twilio programmable messaging, Microsoft Dynamics 365, and a system that adapted to every protocol change

Kaptea designed and deployed a Twilio-powered communications platform integrated with Microsoft Dynamics 365 as the case management CRM. The platform handled outbound notification, inbound response management, and agent-supported follow-up conversations across SMS, voice, and IVR.

16 distinct SMS notification flows covered the full contact tracing journey: test result delivery, close-contact notifications, isolation reminders, follow-up check-ins, escalation paths to a clinical team, and protocol-specific variants as guidance evolved. When the HSE updated guidance, the corresponding flow was updated and back in production within hours, without taking the rest of the platform offline.

16 SMS notification flows on Twilio

From test result delivery to close-contact notification to isolation follow-up, each flow built on Twilio's programmable messaging APIs. Templates, variables, and routing logic were configurable without rebuilding the platform.

Microsoft Dynamics 365 CRM integration

Outbound notifications and inbound responses both wrote into Microsoft Dynamics 365, giving the HSE's case-management teams a single source of truth for every contact. Tracers worked from one system, not many.

IVR for tracer-supported follow-up

Inbound calls routed through an IVR layer that handed callers to the right tracing team based on their stage in the journey. The IVR flows changed daily as protocols evolved; the system kept up.

National-scale resilience

Built and operated on Twilio's carrier-grade infrastructure with cross-channel failover, the platform scaled with case volume rather than capping it. Audit logs and message-level evidence available throughout for regulatory review.

Results

6.9 million messages, delivered without breaking

Across the duration of the contact tracing programme, Kaptea's platform delivered 6,923,928 text messages to the population of Ireland. Test results, close-contact notifications, isolation reminders, follow-up check-ins, all dispatched through 16 distinct SMS flows, integrated with the Microsoft Dynamics 365 case-management estate, supporting clinicians and contact tracers in real time.
