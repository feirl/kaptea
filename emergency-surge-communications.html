The challenge

When the surge hits, every contact has to land and every reply has to be resolved.

01

Surge needs more than a steady-state API

A recall instruction lands at 9am with a regulatory deadline at 5pm, and you need to load 50,000 contacts and dispatch within the hour. Kaptea gives you CSV upload, an API trigger or a live CRM connection, so the send goes out the moment the instruction does.

02

Regulatory windows run on a clock

A vehicle warranty notice or a drug recall under MHRA or CMA timelines carries a regulatory deadline. Hitting that window cleanly, dispatched in minutes with a full audit trail behind it, is the difference between a clean response and a conversation with the regulator.

03

Mass outbound is half the job. The inbound is the other half.

50,000 messages go out and thousands of replies come back: outage queries, recall enquiries, return requests, eligibility questions. Conversational AI resolves the inbound in natural language and routes the complex cases to a live agent, so the response keeps moving when it matters most.

04

Branded calling is what gets answered

Pickup rates for unknown numbers sit below 30 percent. Kaptea places branded, verified calls that customers recognise and answer, so the urgent message lands and the legal obligation is met.

Urgent messaging and recall: what you need to know

Kaptea's urgent messaging platform is used across pharmaceuticals and medical devices (drug and device recalls under MHRA and EMA requirements), automotive (vehicle warranty and safety recalls), utilities and energy (outage notifications under CRU and Ofgem obligations), financial services (urgent client notifications under FCA and CBI conduct rules), and public sector (citizen communications for government bodies and health services). The platform is compliance-first across all of these: dedicated instance, per-message immutable audit trail, EU/UK/US data residency.
A recall campaign starts with a CSV upload or an API trigger from your OEM feed, CRM, or recall management system. Pronto validates the data, maps fields to your template variables, and dispatches the first wave on the configured channel (typically email). A configurable wait window then triggers SMS to non-openers, and a branded call to anyone still unreached. The AI absorbs predictable inbound replies (is my product affected, how do I return it, when will I be contacted). Complex or sensitive cases route to a live agent with full conversation context. Every step is logged with channel, timestamp, and delivery confirmation, and the full campaign evidence is exportable for regulator submission.
Yes. Pronto's CSV upload and campaign builder are designed for compliance, ops, and recall team leads who need to act fast without raising a development ticket. Upload the affected customer list, select the template, configure the escalation logic, validate, and dispatch. The same workflow runs whether the list is 500 or 500,000. IT involvement is required once, at deployment, to provision the dedicated instance and activate channel access. After that, campaigns run from the UI with no engineering dependency.
A cascade is the automatic progression through channels per recipient until confirmed delivery. Pronto's default recall cascade sends email first, waits for a defined open-rate window, then sends SMS to non-openers, then triggers a branded call to anyone still unreached. Each recipient moves through the cascade only as far as needed: if they open the email, the SMS is suppressed. The cascade matters for recalls because regulators do not accept "we sent an email" as sufficient evidence that a safety communication was delivered. A cascade with per-recipient delivery confirmation, across multiple channels, provides the evidence that satisfies the question.
Branded calling shows your verified business name and logo on the recipient's phone screen when you call them, instead of an unknown number or a generic caller ID. Industry data from Twilio Branded Calling deployments shows pickup rates lift 30 to 50 percent compared with unbranded outbound calls. For recall and urgent regulatory communications, that difference can determine whether the message lands inside the regulatory deadline. A missed call on a recall is a missed legal obligation.
For every campaign, Pronto produces a per-recipient audit log covering: message sent (channel, timestamp, template version), delivery confirmation (carrier acknowledgement per channel), open or engagement where technically available (email open, SMS click), any inbound reply and its resolution (AI-handled or agent-escalated), and confirmed closure status per case. Template changes are logged with user, timestamp, and approval record. The full dataset is exportable in standard formats for regulator submission. This applies whether the campaign is a pharmaceutical recall, a vehicle safety notice, or an urgent regulatory notification to financial services customers.
