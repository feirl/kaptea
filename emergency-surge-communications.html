The challenge

Even mature messaging stacks fail when the clock starts and the volume is high.

01

CSV upload doesn't exist in mature comms stacks

The largest, most mature messaging customers still don't have a way to load 50,000 contacts and dispatch in the next hour. APIs are great for steady-state. They're the wrong tool for a recall instruction that arrives at 9am with a regulatory deadline at 5pm.

02

Recall windows are narrow

A vehicle warranty notice or a pharmaceutical drug recall has a regulatory clock. Manual export, paste, and send is too slow, and every step adds error risk that you'll have to explain to the regulator afterwards.

03

No fallback when the first message fails

Email gets sent. Half don't open. Then nothing. Someone manually pulls the non-openers, exports them, starts an SMS run, and by the time it lands the window has closed. Most stacks treat each channel as a separate campaign, not a sequence.

04

Untrusted caller IDs kill follow-up

Customers don't pick up unknown numbers. Pickup rates collapse below 30 percent, and the urgent message never lands. For recall comms, a missed call is a missed legal obligation.
