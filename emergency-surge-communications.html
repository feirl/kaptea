Why most communications platforms fail at the worst possible moment

01

IVR systems collapse under surge call volume

When a major storm, outage, or emergency event triggers a sudden spike in inbound calls, most IVR platforms aren't built for it. Queues overflow, calls drop, customers can't get through. Operations teams are left managing an infrastructure failure on top of the original incident — exactly when reliable communications matter most.

02

Single-carrier SMS throttles on high-volume sends

Emergency outbound notifications — storm alerts, evacuation notices, service disruption messages — often need to reach millions of recipients in minutes. Single-carrier SMS campaigns hit throughput limits fast. Messages queue, arrive hours late, or fail silently. Customers are left uninformed at the exact moment they need to know what's happening and what to do.

03

No failover means a carrier outage silences everything

During a major incident, communications infrastructure is under maximum stress. If the primary SMS provider has an outage — even briefly — thousands or millions of emergency notifications simply don't arrive. With no automatic failover to a secondary carrier or alternative channel, there's no safety net. By the time the issue is noticed and manually resolved, the window to reach customers has closed.

04

Manual processes create dangerous delays

Sending an emergency notification should take seconds. When it requires a comms team member to log into a platform, build a campaign, upload a contact list, and press send — it takes minutes or hours. In an emergency, that lag directly translates to customers making decisions without the information they need: whether to travel, whether they have power, whether their account is safe.

Automatic cross-channel failover — no human step required

Pronto monitors carrier delivery rates continuously. If a primary carrier falls below delivery threshold — due to outage, congestion, or capacity limits — the platform automatically reroutes to a secondary carrier within seconds, with no manual intervention. At the channel level, if SMS fails to deliver for a recipient, the message automatically cascades to WhatsApp, Email, or RCS. Every failover event is logged with full diagnostic detail. Load shedding is the controlled prioritisation of traffic when a system is under stress.
